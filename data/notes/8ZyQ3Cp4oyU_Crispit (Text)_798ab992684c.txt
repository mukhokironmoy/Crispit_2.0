• **AI and Cybersecurity**
◦ AI will not replace cybersecurity professionals.
◦ People are needed to understand, manage, and work with AI for it to function correctly.
◦ AI can make processes like data protection and access management more efficient, but human oversight is crucial.
◦ The T app example shows that even AI-generated code needs human security expertise.
◦ AI will likely create more cybersecurity jobs, especially in securing AI applications.

• **The Role of a Cyber Content Creator**
◦ Cyber content creators educate the public about cybersecurity.
◦ Content can be technical, fun, or a mix, depending on the creator's purpose.
◦ The field is broad, offering many opportunities for content creation.

• **Platforms and Content Strategy**
◦ Short-form video platforms like TikTok and Instagram are effective for engaging the general public.
◦ Short-form content is efficient, catchy, and good for storytelling.
◦ LinkedIn is also a valuable platform for content creators.
◦ Consistency is key, with a focus on posting regularly.
◦ Trending audios can significantly boost video reach and follower growth.
◦ Editing directly within the app you wish to go viral on can be beneficial.
◦ To go viral, create content relatable to a broad audience, even if it is cybersecurity-related.

• **Motivation and Purpose**
◦ Understand your "why" before starting content creation.
◦ Initial motivations included inspiring more people and women into cyber and STEM fields.
◦ The main goal evolved to educating the public on everyday cyber safety tips.
◦ Cyber security is essential for everyone, given pervasive technology.
◦ Simple cyber routines, like checking app permissions, can significantly reduce online risk.

• **Challenges of Content Creation**
◦ Negative comments are common on social media.
◦ It's helpful to view negative comments as driving engagement.
◦ Focusing on the positive interactions and avoiding reading harsh comments can protect mental well-being.
◦ Insecurities, especially regarding intelligence in a technical field, can be triggered by critical comments.
◦ Having an "alter ego" or a separate online persona can help overcome initial embarrassment and judgment from peers or employers.

• **Caitlyn's Journey and Advice**
◦ Caitlyn started by posting three times a day for a year with specific mission-driven statements.
◦ She gained significant traction after a few months of consistent posting.
◦ Her content led to a role at TikTok in cyber security advocacy and culture.
◦ She eventually chose to focus full-time on content creation due to time constraints and a conflict of interest at TikTok.
◦ Don't quit your day job until you reach specific follower milestones.
◦ Have a clear mission, define your brand, set goals, and post consistently.
◦ Don't rewatch your videos after posting; focus on creating new content.
