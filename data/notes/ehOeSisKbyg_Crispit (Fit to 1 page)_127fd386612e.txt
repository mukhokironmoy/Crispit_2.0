### US Waiver for Indian Oil Purchases

- The US issued a statement giving India a 30-day waiver to purchase Russian oil without additional 25% tariffs.
- US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent tweeted about this temporary waiver, stating it supports global oil markets and is a short-term measure.
- The waiver only authorizes transactions involving Russian oil already "stranded at sea."
- US expects India to ramp up purchases of US oil after this 30-day period.
- This "stop-gap measure" aims to alleviate pressure from Iran's attempts to take global energy hostage.
- The move has been criticized by some in India, questioning US authority over India's oil sources.

### India's Oil Procurement Stance

- India had never stopped purchasing oil from Russia despite US threats of tariffs and sanctions.
- While some private Indian companies reduced Russian oil intake, government-owned companies continued purchases.
- The US waiver is seen as a way for the Trump administration to reduce internal pressure.
- US media (Bloomberg, New York Times, Washington Post) are reporting on India's increased Russian oil purchases.
- India is expected to significantly increase Russian oil purchases due to disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz.
- India plans to refine Russian oil and re-export it to Europe, which also faces supply issues from the Strait of Hormuz.
- This situation puts the US in a difficult position, as it aims to limit Russian oil revenues while India continues to purchase it.
- The 30-day waiver may be extended if the US-Iran conflict prolongs.

### US-Iran Conflict and its Implications

- The US-Iran conflict is in its seventh day, with Iran showing no signs of backing down or surrendering.
- Iran has been bombing Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, and Azerbaijan.
- Iran has threatened the US with "painful blows" and recently destroyed a US oil tanker.
- Iran claims to possess new, unseen weapons.
- Iranian Foreign Minister states Tehran is ready for a US ground invasion, expressing confidence in confronting US forces.
- CNN reports suggest no panic in Iran; shops are open, supplies are stocked, and fuel is readily available.
- This suggests either a US miscalculation in attacking Iran without a clear endgame, or a prolonged conflict.
- A prolonged conflict would likely lead to continuous extensions of the 30-day waiver for India.
- The US is spending $1-2 billion daily on the Iran conflict, with $14 billion already spent in seven days.
- The financial burden of this war will fall on American taxpayers.
