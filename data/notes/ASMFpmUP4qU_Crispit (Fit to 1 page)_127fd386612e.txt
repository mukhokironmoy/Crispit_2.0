# INDIA SAVED IRANIAN WARSHIP FROM USA | Iran Thanks India | Guardian of the Indian Ocean
## Link to video: https://youtu.be/ASMFpmUP4qU

### Iran Thanks India
- Iran thanked India for providing humanitarian support to its warship, IRIS Lavan.
- The IRIS Lavan is currently docked safely at Kochi port in India.
- If the ship left Indian waters, it would likely be targeted and sunk by U.S. submarines.
- This act highlights the friendly ties between India and Iran.

### IRIS Dena Incident
- The U.S. recently sank another Iranian warship, IRIS Dena, near the coasts of India and Sri Lanka.
- This incident led to criticism of India, particularly from Middle Eastern news outlets like Al Jazeera, for its "Guardian of the Indian Ocean" claim.
- Critics argued that India failed to protect an Iranian ship in its claimed area of influence.
- India clarified that its role as a "Guardian" is to provide humanitarian aid in crises, not to intervene in conflicts between two nations.
- India had offered shelter to IRIS Dena at Visakhapatnam after it participated in the Milan naval exercise.
- IRIS Dena declined the offer, sailed into international waters, and was subsequently sunk by a U.S. submarine.
- 87 crew members died; others were rescued by the Sri Lankan Navy, with Indian assistance.

### India's Justification for Providing Shelter
- India sheltered IRIS Lavan on humanitarian grounds.
- The Iranian warships (IRIS Dena and IRIS Lavan) were far from home and not actively involved in attacks.
- Indian External Affairs Minister S. Jaishankar stated, "We did the right thing," emphasizing the humanitarian aspect.
- He explained that the ships arrived for a fleet review before the conflict escalated, effectively getting "caught on the wrong side of events."
- Young cadets from IRIS Lavan have disembarked and are housed in an Indian naval facility.
- India is not allowing these warships to be used for attacking U.S. submarines or bases.
- Neither Israel nor the U.S. government has criticized India's humanitarian decision.

### India as Guardian of the Indian Ocean
- India's action in protecting IRIS Lavan reaffirms its role as the "Guardian of the Indian Ocean."
- This involves providing safe refuge and humanitarian assistance, not military intervention in international conflicts.
- India had offered shelter to both IRIS Dena and IRIS Lavan as early as March 1.
- The ability to protect a foreign warship from external attack while it is in its port demonstrates India's capability as a guardian.
